Our Story

Deep Roots.
A New Standard.

Esoteric Farm traces its lineage to Cameron Equestrian Center, where founder Kersten learned the craft under her mother, Marleta Lillejord. When Marleta passed unexpectedly in 2013, Kersten carried the legacy forward — rebuilding it as something entirely her own.

Situated on Cameron Rd in Manor, Texas, we serve competitors across Austin, Pflugerville, Westlake, Round Rock, Cedar Park, Leander, and Lakeway. Our program is deliberately boutique — small enough to know every horse and rider; serious enough to take you to the ring ready to win.

Our most celebrated horse, the Grand Prix jumper Entrepreneur, won the World Cup series for the Pacific League with Olympian Greg Best in the irons — a horse Kersten raised and developed from the ground up.

High standards. Low drama. A program that fits your actual life.

The Team

Trainers Who

Consistent coaching, honest feedback, and a relationship built over time — not a revolving door of instruction. You will always know where you stand and what comes next.

Kersten Levine
Owner & Head Trainer · Esoteric Farm

Kersten learned to ride under her mother, Marleta Lillejord, at Cameron Equestrian Center. When Marleta passed unexpectedly in 2013, Kersten reincarnated the program as Esoteric Farm, carrying the same exacting standards into a new era.

She raises and breaks her own horses from birth through the show ring. Her most celebrated horse, Entrepreneur, won the World Cup series for the Pacific League with Olympian Greg Best aboard — a horse Kersten produced entirely from the ground up.

In addition to Esoteric Farm, Kersten serves as Head Trainer at Augusta Pines Farm in Spring, TX — a premier 33-acre Hunter/Jumper facility near The Woodlands. She continues to show actively in National and International Derbys.

Nancy Ansley
Trainer & Barn Manager

Nancy began riding as a child in Phoenix, AZ, competing extensively in hunters, equitation, and jumpers through the Grand Prix level. She earned success aboard her own horses and those she rode for other owners, accumulating numerous championships and multiple State Championships through the Arizona Horse Exhibitors Association.

Holding a Doctor of Veterinary Medicine from Auburn University, Nancy brings a depth of medical judgment that shapes every decision at the farm — from evaluating a prospective horse to recognizing early signs of unsoundness. Clients aren't guessing. They have someone who has studied both sides of the stall door.
